colors that fade gracefully over time

Often, we see organic (synthetic) or hybrid (organic + inorganic) eyebrow pigments leave skin through grey or blue. This happens because there are NO organic browns available on the market – and to create brown, companies mix yellow, red and black (sometimes blue) ink. Hybrid formulations are no different than organic ones, with the exception that they add titanium dioxide or yellow iron oxide (hence the name “hybrid”). Each of the colors that work to make up organic brown has a different life span, however; yellow and red fade much faster than blue or black, and this leaves us with bluish-grey eyebrows.

Many times, we will see inorganic (mineral) pigments leave skin through orange or pink. This happens due to an incorrect undertone of the brown iron oxide. Not all mineral pigments are created equally – there are several methods in which brown pigments are created, and those will determine the undertone of the pigment and long-term color behavior. Nowadays, the vast majority of brown iron oxide on the market has cold or red undertones. Brown mineral pigments with red undertones will leave skin through orange or pink, and those browns with cool undertones will fade through maroon or purple.

Only browns that have yellow undertones will leave skin through natural brown colors. Liquid Powder has created mineral brown pigments with beautiful yellow undertones that will fade gracefully over time.
